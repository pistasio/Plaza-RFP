02

Introduction

The Agora Project proposes the transformation of the newly announced plaza spaces within Khalifa University’s new dormitories [1]. It aims to convert the empty spaces into a cozy, multifunctional community hub. While these areas were originally envisioned as a social space, they lack the right infrastructure to support student engagement. This proposal aims to change that by introducing four features: a community engagement lounge, a dining and convenience store hub, a sustainable micro-mobility station, and reverse vending machines. Together, these elements aim to bring the university closer to its five strategic goals 2024-2028 [2].

03

Literature Review

Studies have shown that student accommodation and its environment play a significant role in the academic growth, wellbeing and overall university satisfaction of the students. Poorly designed dormitories, especially the ones that lack convenient services and social lounges, directly or indirectly increase stress, cause isolation and lower interactions among students [3],[4]. This suggests that besides the basic living needs, students need a friendly and convenient environment that supports their academic and personal development.

A key factor that was found in many studies was the significance of social spaces. Spaces like a lounge along with the student housing have shown increase in peer interactions, formation of a sense of belonging and overall wellbeing [5]. Additionally, access to convenient services like a supermarket or a restaurant influences the daily routine of students and their satisfaction with campus life [6],[7]. When such services are barred for students, they experience inconvenience and reduced productivity.

Furthermore, studies on university campus mobility points out that large university environments can bring challenges in accessibility. So, solutions like e-scooters have been studied and suggest that it improves travel efficiency and saves time as well [8]. Having such a transportation system enhances mobility and campus connectivity.

Finally, research on sustainability implies that incentive-based recycling systems like reverse vending machines increase recycling motives in students. Studies show that if rewards are given for participation in such initiatives, it encourages more students to have a recycling mindset to return recyclable materials [9]. All these findings together support the need for improvement in the dormitory while considering convenience, mobility, social interactions and sustainability.

04

Survey Analysis

To establish the need for this project, a survey was conducted to identify specific gaps in the current dorm experience. The results highlight that while students can order food from outside, it is not the most efficient or convenient option. In fact, among those who answered the food section, 66% reported ordering delivery at least 4 to 6 times every week. This high percentage shows that the current options in the residential area are not meeting daily needs. When asked to choose their top priorities from a list including upgraded lounges, rentable e-scooters, and 24/7 convenience stores, a Restaurant/Dining Hall was the top request, chosen by 72% of all participants, followed closely by the 24/7 Convenience Store at 67%.

Regarding student facilities, the data shows that private study rooms and entertainment options like gaming or pool tables are a major priority, with over 60% of students selecting them as must-have lounge upgrades. Students also suggested improvements for gym facilities and more consistent cleaning services to maintain a better living standard in the residential area. These statistics prove that the proposal is based on the actual preferences of the people living in the dorms. By focusing on these specific resources, the project can deliver an innovative and impactful environment that solves the real convenience and study issues students face every day.

05

Project Description

The Agora Project envisions the redevelopment of the recently announced public spaces in Khalifa University’s new dormitories [10]. It seeks to transform the unused areas into a warm community centre. These spaces were initially intended as public spaces, but they are not currently equipped for student interaction. This leads to them being underused and not contributing to student engagement. The proposal seeks to change this by incorporating four elements: a community engagement lounge, dining and convenience store; a sustainable micro-mobility station; and reverse vending machines. These components will help the university achieve its strategic priorities for 2024-2028, including improving student experience, sustainability and innovation on campus [11].

The activity proposed here is to transform the plazas into vibrant, student-friendly spaces in a two-stage process. The heart of the redesign is the Community Engagement Lounge, a versatile and comfortable space for academic and social activities. The lounge, which will feature flexible furniture, shaded spaces, lighting, and power outlets, will be a daily destination where students can study, work, or socialise. This design element promotes a sense of belonging and community within the dormitories by offering a welcoming and accessible space.

The Dining and Convenience Store Hub is another feature that is complementary to this, providing food and convenience services. This will offer fast, affordable food, snacks and essentials in a small yet efficient format. Operating longer hours and accepting digital payments, this will provide a convenient and efficient option for students in their own residences. This will make life easier and promote increased use of the plaza as a living hub.

The project also includes a sustainable micro-mobility station to enhance the campus' accessibility. This will offer a network of e-scooters and bikes, as well as parking and charging bays. With the assistance of a user-friendly mobile app, students will be able to access these modes of transport for short trips. This not only helps to reduce car dependence but also encourages sustainable transportation, contributing to the overarching sustainability goals.

Lastly, the project includes reverse vending machines (RVMs) as part of its sustainability efforts. The RVM system is an incentive-driven recycling programme that enables students to recycle plastic bottles and cans for rewards, promoting active involvement in recycling. Incentive-based systems have been shown to increase user participation and recycling efficiency compared to conventional systems [12], and campus-based RVMs increase awareness and convenience [11].

This rejuvenation does not only reclaim unused space, but it also improves student satisfaction and belonging and supports Khalifa University's quest for innovation and excellence.

06

Expected Challenges

One major challenge we could face with this project is costs. Everyone would like all these features to be implemented into the new accommodation, however the budget may not be enough to fulfill all the proposed plans, and would require more than one phase to complete. This accommodation is also being marketed as the GCC’s largest student accommodation yet, and living up to that title means that maintenance and operation cost could be very high. Managing a large scale accommodation with multiple services requires continuous funding and staff, and if done incorrectly, can lead to a decline in facilities and student’s daily lives.

Another challenge could be the noise and distractions students may face with these new facilities. With these new facilities such as the lounges, sports areas and social spaces, students could be facing an issue of balancing social life and academic focus, as the implementation of shared spaces encourages more interactions within students, which may lead to an excessive amount of social activity that distract students from their work and could increase stress in the long term if this repeats frequently.

Another challenge we could face is overcrowding and the unequal usage of facilities. Some of the spaces such as gyms or lounges may become overcrowded during peak times, while other facilities may be unused which leads to an inefficiency in using resources. To face this, we could publish polls and surveys for students living in the dorms to help us decide which facilities are the most important in their day to day lives, and allocate the resources accordingly.

07

Conclusion

In conclusion, The Agora Project isn’t about filling up empty spaces, it’s about making the space meaningful and effective, while directly contributing to Khalifa University’s goals and missions of being a more sustainable and innovative institution. By introducing retail stores, cost efficient cafeterias, green transport, and reverse vending machines, these plazas will become an active part of student life. Overall, this proposal is in line with Khalifa University’s vision for innovation and its 2024-2028 strategic goals, while providing a seamless and joyful life for the students in dorms.
